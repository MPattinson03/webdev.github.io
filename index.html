About

Hi! I’m Megan Pattinson, and I’m currently a student teacher at the University of Limerick, pursuing a degree in Mathematics and Computer Science. I’m passionate about blending the world of numbers with the exciting possibilities of technology, all while making learning fun and relatable. My mission as a future teacher is to inspire students to not only grasp complex concepts but to also develop a genuine love for STEM subjects. Whether it’s simplifying tricky equations or making coding feel like second nature, I thrive on creating an inclusive and dynamic classroom. When I’m not buried in textbooks, you’ll likely find me coaching underage GAA or sharing my knowledge through math grinds. I believe teaching is all about growing together, and I’m constantly honing my skills to help students succeed and spark their curiosity!

What i do

Teaching & Pedagogy

As an aspiring educator with a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics and Computer Science, I specialize in creating engaging lesson plans that make complex topics accessible. With experience in teaching both Junior and Leaving Certificate classes, I’m dedicated to fostering critical thinking and a passion for STEM in my students. My teaching philosophy focuses on inclusivity, adaptability, and continuous improvement.

Programming

With strong programming skills in languages like JavaScript, Python, and Java, I focus on practical coding exercises that help students master concepts. I have hands-on experience in web development (HTML, CSS, JavaScript), object-oriented development, and software testing, allowing me to create dynamic learning environments both in and outside the classroom.

Curriculum & Assessment

As part of my degree, I’ve gained in-depth knowledge of curriculum development, assessment practices, and understanding the diverse needs of learners. My experience in curriculum design, along with modules on statistics, algebra, discrete mathematics, and data structures, allows me to create a structured and supportive learning experience that aligns with students' needs and the educational standards.
